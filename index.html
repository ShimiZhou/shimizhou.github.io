About

Background & Interests

I am an Assistant Professor of Information Systems at the Bryan School of Business and Economics, University of North Carolina at Greensboro (UNCG). I received my Ph.D. in Business Administration (Information Systems) from Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) in May 2024, where I also earned my M.S. in Information Technology.

My research explores the intersection of information technology, data science, and social inclusion. I am particularly interested in how technology can shape equitable participation in academia, organizations, and society. I am also interested in emerging areas such as gender equity in academic and professional networks, thought leadership in AI, and the role of data justice in shaping inclusive technologies.

"Technology does not simply mirror society — it actively constructs it. Understanding how IS shapes who gets to collaborate, lead, and belong is both a scholarly and a moral imperative."

Before joining UNCG, I worked as an Associate Data Scientist in Advanced Analytics & AI at John Hancock | Manulife in Boston, bringing an industry lens to my academic work. I was also a Research Assistant on the NSF ADVANCE ImPACT IT project, focused on increasing the participation and advancement of women in information technology.

Research

Focus Areas & Projects

Inclusive & AI-Ready Smart Cities

Examining how smart city technologies can equitably serve all residents — including people with disabilities — and how municipalities can build genuine AI readiness. Work spans a systematic literature review (EJIS) and a maturity model & assessment toolkit to help city managers move toward responsible AI deployment in urban governance.

Smart CitiesAccessibilityAI MaturityValue Co-creationGovernance

Gender Equity in Academic Networks

Using social network analysis to study collaboration patterns, homophily, and research impact among IS scholars. Work examines how gender shapes co-authorship, institutional ties, and career outcomes in the IS academic community.

Gender EquitySocial Network AnalysisHomophilyBibliometrics

AI Thought Leadership & Decision-Making

Analysing leadership discourse around generative AI using text analytics and NLP. Current work examines how executives and academics frame AI decision-making, and what this reveals about power, authority, and responsibility.

Generative AILeadership DiscourseNLPText Analytics
Ongoing — Stay Tuned

Societal Impact of Generative AI

A new research direction exploring the broader societal consequences of generative AI — including effects on equity, labour, knowledge production, and social participation. Projects are underway. More to come soon.

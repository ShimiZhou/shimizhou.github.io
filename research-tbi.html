Deciphering Traumatic Brain Injury: How Data-Driven Decision Aids Can Make a Difference

Research Post  ·  University of North Carolina at Greensboro
HealthcareUX DesignHTMLJavaScriptDecision Support

Every year, millions of individuals are affected by Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI). For patients and their families, navigating the sea of medical decisions can be overwhelming. Data science offers an opportunity to make that decision-making more transparent, informed, and patient-centric.

The Complexity of TBI Decisions

Imagine being at a crossroads with multiple paths ahead, but without a map to guide your way. That's how many feel when making decisions about TBI treatments and interventions. Every patient's situation is unique, requiring individualized assessments and care plans. The challenge: how can we streamline this decision-making process to benefit both medical professionals and patients?

Introducing the TBI Decision Aid

Our mission was clear — to develop a tool that makes TBI decisions more understandable and tailored to each patient's situation. Achieving this required harnessing the power of data at every stage:

  • Gathering Insights: Before diving into design, we initiated offline surveys, reaching out to patients' families at the Hospital of the University of Massachusetts. This provided a direct window into their genuine needs, expectations, and concerns.
  • Model Validation: Leveraging medical history data from over 100 patients, I ensured that our tool was not just user-friendly, but also medically sound. This rigorous validation process was instrumental in anticipating user needs.

Seamless User Interface

More than just a decision-making tool, we envisioned the platform as a companion for TBI families. The technical implementation drew on:

  • HTML5 & JavaScript for an intuitive, responsive, and accessible interface
  • Iterative design enhancements based on continuous user feedback
  • Patient-centered UX principles to ensure users felt guided, informed, and empowered at every step

Takeaways

This project demonstrated how thoughtful application of data science and UX design can reduce the cognitive burden on patients and families during one of the most stressful periods of their lives. It also reinforced the value of co-design — bringing together patients, families, and clinicians early in the design process to build tools that truly serve their needs.
